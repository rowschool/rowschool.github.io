### STEM Education

The feed must be relevant, at least some of the time. Most of these focus on STEM education, but a few of these have a slightly broader view of education, and these have a lot of other great information for those in the education field
They sites must be active. In other words, they should have someone posting to them on a regular basis, not just once a month or less. (https://www.theedadvocate.org/edvocates-list-24-must-follow-stem-education-twitter-feeds/)

Look into this further: https://www.bestcollegesonline.com/blog/50-essential-twitter-feeds-for-stem-educators/

@TeachingSTEM: Provides readers with articles on science, tech, engineering, and math.
@stemschools: Gives readers great tips on promoting STEM education.
@STEMschool: Disseminates excellent tips on how to teach a STEM subject.
@SciAfterSchool: A great place to find STEM resources.
@crsscience: Want to liven up your curriculum? This Twitter feed can help.
@STEMAhead: Follow this feed to learn about STEM Ahead, and their beautiful programming.
@stemnetwork: Educator schools twitter about STEM resources and initiatives.
@NSTA: The NSTA is the go-to place for resources related teaching STEM in the classroom
@STEM_Works: Ran by SMU, this resource helps teachers to find information on STEM instruction.
@leache: Edward Leach shares STEM-related news.
@STEMConnector: Tweets out STEM-related updates, articles, and resources.
@MITK12STEM: MIT-backed Twitter feed that will keep you up to date on all things STEM.
@changeequation: This feed is all about encouraging STEM literacy while providing you up to date STEM resources.
@ConnectMinds: A Wonderful community whose mission is to get more kids involved in STEM.
@almostrocketsci: Encourages kids to consider careers in the STEM fields, and provides them the resources to do just that.
@exploratorium: Allows children to explore a career in STEM, and provides educators with up to date news and resources.
@STEMChallenge: Uses video games to get kids involved in STEM.
@egfi: Allows children to explore a career in STEM, while providing them with information about careers in STEM.
@ScienceCenters: Tweets out pertinent information about science and technology centers across the nation.
@OppEquation: Ensures that kids underrepresented groups are exposed to STEM careers.
@SciGirls: Helps girls explore a career in STEM.
@cstemorg: Helps minority groups learn more about careers in STEM.
@NCWIT: Devoted to helping more girls and women to choose STEM careers.
@womenintech: Showcases women in tech careers, offers advice to women interested in tech careers and encourages young girls to become involved in tech.
